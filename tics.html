What are Cyber Security Threats?

Threats to cybersecurity are actions taken by people with bad intentions with the aim of stealing data, harming computing systems, or disrupting them. We go into greater depth on each of these common sorts of cyber threats below, including malware, social engineering, man-in-the-middle (MitM) attacks, denial of service (DoS), and injection assaults.

Cyber threats can come from a wide range of sources, including rogue governments and terrorist organizations, lone hackers, and legitimate users like workers or contractors who use their positions of trust to commit evil deeds.
